<p><p>Low Temperature Fuel Cells (LTFC) are electrochemical devices that convert chemical energy from fuels, typically hydrogen, into electricity at relatively low operational temperatures, usually below 120 degrees Celsius. LTFCs are primarily used in applications such as portable power devices, backup power systems, and automotive fuel cells, contributing to the transition towards cleaner energy sources.</p><p>The LTFC market is poised for significant growth, with an expected CAGR of 5% during the forecast period. Key trends include advancements in membrane technology and improvements in fuel cell durability and performance.
