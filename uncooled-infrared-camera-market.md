<p><p>The uncooled infrared camera market is divided into three main types based on wavelength. Uncooled long-wave infrared (LWIR) cameras operate in the 8-14 micrometer range and are commonly used for thermal imaging in various applications. Uncooled middle-wave infrared (MWIR) cameras function in the 3-5 micrometer range, offering high-resolution imaging suitable for industrial and military uses. Uncooled short-wave infrared (SWIR) cameras, covering 1-3 micrometers, excel in applications involving material analysis and surveillance, providing enhanced visibility in low-light conditions.</p></p>
