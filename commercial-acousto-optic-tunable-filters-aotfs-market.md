<p><p>Commercial Acousto-Optic Tunable Filters (AOTFs) are optical devices that utilize sound waves in a medium to selectively filter light based on wavelength. The market for AOTFs is primarily divided into two types: collinear AOTFs, where the acoustic and optical beams are aligned, allowing for efficient wavelength tuning and compact designs; and noncollinear AOTFs, where the beams intersect at an angle, providing broader spectral tuning capabilities. Each type caters to various applications in scientific research, telecommunications, and medical imaging.</p></p>
