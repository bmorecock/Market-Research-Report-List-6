<p><ul><li>Oligonucleotide DNA Microarrays (oDNA)</li><li>Complementary DNA Microarrays (cDNA)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Microarrays in agriculture are vital tools for analyzing gene expression and genetic variation in plants. Oligonucleotide DNA microarrays (oDNA) utilize short DNA sequences to probe specific genes, enabling precise gene identification and quantification. Complementary DNA microarrays (cDNA) involve synthesizing DNA from mRNA, allowing researchers to assess active gene expression in response to environmental conditions. Both types facilitate advancements in crop improvement, pest resistance, and overall plant health, playing a crucial role in biotechnology and precision agriculture.</p></p>
